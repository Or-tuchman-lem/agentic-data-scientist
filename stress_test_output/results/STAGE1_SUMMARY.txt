6. PRELIMINARY OBSERVATIONS FOR MODELING
================================================================================

Strong Signals Identified:

1. Treatment Variant Choice Matters Significantly
   - 52.5% performance difference between best and worst
   - Current production is sub-optimal (14th of 27)
   - Low coinsurance (70%) unexpectedly performs better

2. Premium Price Sensitivity
   - Purchased customers saw similar premiums to non-purchased
   - Premium distribution overlap suggests price is not the only factor
   - Need to analyze price elasticity by customer segment

3. Network Effects May Be Important
   - Connected users and multi-pet discounts show promise
   - These features warrant deeper investigation

4. Pet and Customer Characteristics
   - 399 unique breeds - may need grouping/encoding
   - Pet age distribution shows concentration in 0-5 years
   - State-level variations exist (income, vet clinics)

Potential Challenges:

1. Class Imbalance: 85% non-purchase vs 15% purchase
   - Will need stratified sampling and appropriate metrics
   - Consider SMOTE or class weights for modeling

2. High Cardinality Features:
   - PET_BREED_CLEAN: 399 categories
   - STATE: 41 categories
   - May need feature engineering (grouping, embeddings)

3. Temporal Patterns:
   - Quotes span ~7 months
   - Need to check for seasonal trends
   - Consider time-based validation split

4. Feature Engineering Needed:
   - Price/income ratio
   - Pet age bins
   - Breed groupings (by risk/popularity)
   - Geographic clusters
